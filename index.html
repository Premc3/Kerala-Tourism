About

National Geographic's Traveller magazine listed Kerala in the 'Ten Paradises of the World' and '50 must visit places of a lifetime'. Being a coastal region rich in greenery and with a unique culture, the state has attracted tourists from across the globe since time immemorial. Wedged between Arabian Sea in the West and Western Ghats in the East geographically Kerala is unique and beautiful. This unique geography of Kerala provides many hills, high mountains and valleys, dense forests along with the beautiful water bodies’ backwaters, lakes and beaches. Being encroached upon by the Western Ghats Eastern Kerala holds so many mountains and valleys. This is the place where there are so many tea, coffee and spice plantations. Major Attractions


Trivandrum

Trivandrum is the capital city of Kerala and is one of the largest cities in the state.

Alappuzha

Alappuzha is a city on the Laccadive Sea in the southern Indian state of Kerala.

Kottayam

Kottayam is flanked by the Western Ghats on the east and the Vembanad Lake and paddy fields of Kuttanad on the west.

Visit
Kozhikode

Kozhikode is a coastal city in the south Indian state of Kerala. It was a significant spice trade center and is close to Kappad Beach.

Visit
Wayanad

Wayanad is a rural district in Kerala. In the east, the Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary is a lush, forested region with areas of high altitude.
